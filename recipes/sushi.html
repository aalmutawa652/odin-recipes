Sushi

Description

The California Roll is a popular sushi roll made with imitation crab, creamy avocado, and crisp cucumber, all wrapped in seasoned rice and seaweed. Known for its inside-out style, where rice is on the outside, it's often finished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds or tobiko (fish roe). This Western-style sushi is perfect for beginners—mild in flavor, easy to love, and no raw fish required!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sushi rice (cooked and seasoned)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed)
  • 2-3 sticks imitation crab meat
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise (optional)
  • Sesame seeds or tobiko (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Place a bamboo sushi mat on a clean surface and cover it with plastic wrap.
  2. Lay a sheet of nori, shiny side down, on the mat.
  3. Spread an even layer of sushi rice over the nori, then sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  4. Flip the nori so the rice is facing down.
  5. Place imitation crab, avocado slices, and cucumber strips along the bottom edge of the nori.
  6. Using the mat, carefully roll the sushi away from you, pressing gently to shape it.
  7. Once rolled, use a sharp knife to slice the roll into 6–8 pieces.
  8. Serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi if desired.
